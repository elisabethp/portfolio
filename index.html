bubble-sort vs selection-sort

To scrape the world surrounding space and time complexities of algorithms, I conducted an experiment testing the efficiencies of bubble-sort versus selection-sort. My results found bubble-sort to be more effective when an array of randomly generated numbers was small. Selection-sort far surpassed bubble-sort when it came to larger arrays. I attributed this difference to the amount of “writes” and “reads“ bubble-sort performs in relation to selection-sort.
